Desperation measures from some Americans really started to show during the Crumbs administration. It was not only at the federal level. Local and state jurisdictions also adapted to the issues of dissolution of trade agreements, shuttering of universities, and reshoring multinational corporations. General background on the economic conditions can be found in [A general description of the American economy under fictional American President Crumbs](https://github.com/Changissnz/the_crumbs_economy_made_trenches__claude_generated).

During a period of three years under Crumbs, around a full 40% of able-bodied American citizens — many of them college graduates — could not find any work to do. They could do independent work, mainly alongside A.I. LLM systems. But most of them would not be compensated for it. Some were simply short out of luck. The A.I. business, due to its massive infrastructural demands, was already undergoing nationalization before Crumbs took office. When Crumbs took office, the A.I. business he was pushing was state-owned corporation for the masses, while the rich could throw their money into a few megacorporations for ghastly returns. The laissez faire that Crumbs had promised to his wealthy backers was fully delivered for certain industries, the LLM industry especially. It just was not feasible for individuals without existing wealth to compete with these megacorporations. Political action committees would have tried to blacklist them. If blacklisting did not work, lawyers and investigators would play a long game of finding legal loopholes. Those individuals would always have to feel vulnerable and conscientious about their actions. The message was clear: do your own thing, our A.I. systems can retrieve all your digital work through the A.I. network, we can lie and steal if the federal government does not care, we answer to shareholders, and there might be another great war in the next decade or so that the United States takes part in.

Austerity had become the operating principle not by design but by exhaustion. There was simply less of everything — less funding, less institutional support, less patience for anything that did not produce immediate returns.

There were complaints about the Crumbs administration catering to extremists, mainly of Brittania and Germania. Despite how much help his decentralized federal government, through their lack of resistance and deterrence, could have provided to these groups, this certainly did not help the public image of Brittania — the ethno-realm experiencing the steepest decline in power, comparatively with the other three: Hispania, Gaullia, and Germania.

Germania had been able to maintain a constant during this time period. Since Brittania had separated from the Western European pact, Germania had to take up more responsibility. It served as referee and arms manufacturer for the Schlavian Civil War, an initiative that alone provided more than enough work for its populace.

Hispania had taken an opposite strategy to that of Brittania. Members such as Jhessay Hessianez, as discussed in [A description of two fictional female characters that exemplifies the axial transition of Western power from Brittania to Hispania](https://github.com/Changissnz/more_on_the_societal_issues_under_the_crumbs_administration__claude_generated), had preferred to take sure and steady steps toward their objectives, avoiding reaching out of their depths and striving out of excess. The result was measured advancements that were difficult to fully push back against, especially since Brittania at that point was surely not able to even stabilize its own security. Issues such as food shortages, a recent famine that cut off trade to large portions of Schinia, and cultural matters of feminism and low birth rates pushed Brittania further into an austerity it had not formally declared but was visibly practicing — cutting quietly where it once spent openly.

On the matter of Jhessay Hessianez and Jhennah Sutterson, the description of the differences between the two's demeanors contradicts characteristics presented about Hispania and Brittania from [A description of the fictionalized Brittania, in the timeframe around the end of the first half of the 21st century](https://github.com/Changissnz/brittania-s_bots_at_your_service__claude_generated). But these contradictions are similar in the sense of macroeconomic principles being different from microeconomic principles. If the world had more Jhennah Suttersons, the world would probably be set aflame due to too much radiance from them. If instead the world had more Jhessay Hessianezes, the strange kind of calculating repression and patience would risk throwing the world back into a dark place. Given the proportions of Jhennah Suttersons and Jhessay Hessianezes of Brittania and Hispania respectively, the numbers output the logic of Brittania's coldness as a collective, despite its individual radiance, and Hispania's collective warmth, despite its cold and externally undeveloped individualism. These two orthodoxies played into the developing Western trends.

Isolationism was not a policy that many of the liberal American leaders of that era thought appropriate. There would just be more menial work for Americans to take up. Isolating America would enable the enemies of America to establish stronger connections. Schinia had already allocated significant manpower and capital towards sustained efforts in developing a new Silk Road connecting its many provinces to Arabia. These efforts were not fully welcome by American business leaders, so there were challenges. But the Western challenge was not exactly fit to take direct military action against Schinia. The wars in Arabia had cost plenty already — expenses that the American populace spun around as more healthcare and socialism needed. Those strains had encouraged American isolationist policy that spottily seeped its way into Western foreign policy overall. The media headlines, for the first couple of years during Crumbs' rule, read: America on the Retreat. Allies Need to Step Up.

Austerity and retreat had become interchangeable terms in the Western vocabulary. The pullback was financial as much as it was military and diplomatic.

---

During the second half of the Crumbs administration, matters were not on the brink. There were no major famines. But there were precautions about the deteriorating state of American infrastructure in certain areas, as investments piled into the A.I. industry while cities were not exactly in the best of conditions. Anger was just as much fanned as it rested on rationality. The complaints that made the headlines and social media seemed to point more to generational problems. The elderly wanted healthcare and better socialist policies, as those programs were cut or at risk of being cut by the federal administration. Those in their late twenties and early thirties wanted stable jobs. People in high places warned of the national debt. Venture capitalists sounded alarms on the massive ripples from A.I. that could trigger a lot of upending change.

Basic municipal services had thinned out in ways that residents noticed before officials acknowledged. Corporations had moved into the gaps, sponsoring parks, road maintenance, and in some cases school programs, attaching their branding to what had previously been considered public goods. Nobody discussed whether this was a reasonable arrangement. It simply became the arrangement.

These broad categories of phenomena were not exactly cause for festivity or for decline. Some issues, a part of life, cannot be fixed by political policy.
